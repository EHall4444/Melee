# Melee Event Taxonomy — Global Edition

**Status:** Social-First Event Analysis | 50+ Real-World Events  
**Scope:** English-speaking world (US, UK, CAN, AUS, NZ, etc.)  
**Filter:** Events with proven watch-party culture + social chatter  
**Date:** May 17, 2026

---

## WHAT MAKES AN EVENT "MELEE-WORTHY"?

**Three non-negotiables:**
1. **Outcome uncertainty** — Result isn't predetermined
2. **Social chatter** — People talk about it during/after (Twitter/TikTok/Discord blows up)
3. **Watch-party culture** — People gather to experience it together (bars, homes, watch parties)

**Secondary signals:**
- Multiple outcomes within one event (stages, rounds, categories)
- Staggered results (drama extends across time)
- Friend groups with different expertise (casual fan + expert = fun dynamic)
- Bragging rights matter (people care about being right)

## KEY INSIGHTS

### 1. **Social Chatter > Viewership Numbers**
- Eurovision (180M viewers) has LESS social chatter density than The Traitor (1.5M viewers)
- Love Island's 3M viewers generate more tweets/second than Super Bowl's 115M because of younger, more online audience
- Implication: Target events with passionate, online-first audiences, not just raw viewership

### 2. **Watch-Party Culture is Essential**
- Events with strong watch-party culture (Super Bowl, Oscars, Love Island, Eurovision) have higher engagement
- Events watched solo (golf, baseball, some reality) have lower group prediction stakes
- Implication: Melee is inherently social; events requiring group viewing are better fits

### 3. **Staggered Outcomes > Single Outcomes**
- 21 Tour stages > 1 Super Bowl winner (in terms of Melee replayability)
- 13 Survivor episodes > 1 winner announcement (drama extends across weeks)
- Implication: Multi-stage/multi-round events drive more engagement

### 4. **Episodic Format Unlocks Weekly Engagement**
- The Traitor (12 weeks of episodes) keeps players engaged longer than single-night Oscars
- Love Island (40+ daily episodes) creates daily Melee participation habits
- Implication: Episodic events create recurring engagement loops

### 5. **Predictability Sweet Spot**
- Events that are TOO predictable (Netflix film releases) don't drive social chatter
- Events that are TOO unpredictable (coin flips) don't drive engagement
- Melee events thrive in "skilled unpredictability" zone: expert knowledge matters, but upsets happen
- Examples: Tour de France (cycling experts have edge, but crashes change everything), Survivor (game knowledge matters, but personalities trump strategy)

## WHAT THIS TAXONOMY TELLS MELEE

### Missing Event Categories to Consider:

1. **Multi-Match Sports Days** — e.g., Test cricket day-to-day, golf tournament day-by-day
   - Similar to Tour stages, but typically within single competition
   - Could be linked Melees (Day 1 Melee, Day 2 Melee, aggregated championship)

2. **Conditional Predictions** — e.g., "If Team A wins this match, predict winner of next match"
   - Requires preview predictions based on uncertain prerequisites
   - America's Cup model: CSS seeds ACM contenders
   - Feature: Melee Progression (already identified in simulations)

3. **Head-to-Head Predictive** — e.g., Sports league weekly games
   - NFL/Premier League have weekly matchups where you predict outcomes
   - Less "single Melee" more "Melee league season"
   - Feature: Season-long Melees with weekly resets or aggregation

4. **Personality-Driven Elimination** — e.g., Big Brother, The Ultimatum, Love is Blind
   - Elimination driven by personality/relationship, not skill
   - High emotional investment, highly social
   - Traitor model applies: episodic, elimination-based

5. **Comparative Rankings** — e.g., "Best Oscars dress," "Best Super Bowl halftime," "Best basketball dunk"
   - Not strictly competitive, but preference-based voting
   - Could work with heavy use of prop questions
   - Feature: Prop-question-first format (currently prop is secondary)

## CONCLUSION

Melee's architecture can handle **all 50 events in this taxonomy** with only two new features:
1. **Linked Melees** (Phase 1.5) — Aggregates multi-event scores

Tour de France, The Traitor, Love Island, and Eurovision are more Melee-native than Super Bowl or World Cup, despite lower raw viewership.
